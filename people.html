People

Lab director

Hadar Fisher, PhD

Principal Investigator

Dr. Fisher is a clinical psychologist and an incoming Assistant Professor in the Department of Psychology at Bar-Ilan University. She received her PhD from Bar-Ilan University, completed her first postdoctoral fellowship at the University of Haifa as part of the Data Science Research Center, and her second at Harvard Medical School and McLean Hospital.

Her research examines the role of emotions in the etiology and treatment of depression. Her interests include affect dynamics and emotion regulation in daily life, emotional processes and mechanisms of change in psychotherapy, adolescent depression, and computational approaches to mental health, including natural language processing, large language models (LLMs), machine learning, facial expression analysis, and passive smartphone sensing. Her work has appeared in Nature, Nature Reviews Psychology, npj Digital Medicine, Emotion, Clinical Psychological Science, and Behaviour Research and Therapy, among others.

Lab members

Gilor Friedman-Zeltzer

PhD Student

Gilor Friedman-Zeltzer is a doctoral student. She is a specialist clinical psychologist and a certified Unified Protocol (UP) trainer and supervisor through the Unified Protocol Institute (UPI). Alongside her private practice, she provides UP-based supervision at a trauma recovery center. She earned her M.A. in Clinical Psychology with honors from Ruppin Academic Center, following graduate training in educational counseling at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. Her doctoral research examines therapists' emotional experiences as mechanisms of therapeutic change, specifically how therapists' moment-to-moment emotional trajectories and their ability to move flexibly across emotional states shape patients' emotional processing and treatment outcomes. She combines automated facial-expression analysis, structured video-recall measures of therapist affect, and large language models to capture emotional processing from session transcripts.

Shiri Reznik

PhD Student

Shiri Reznik is an incoming Ph.D. student. Her research interests center on interpersonal dynamics and the use of innovative methods to understand how social interactions shape psychological well-being. She earned her B.A. in Behavioral Sciences from Ben-Gurion University and her M.A. in Clinical Psychology from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. Her master's research examined idiographic processes within romantic couples' conflicts. Her doctoral research will use passive sensing and intensive longitudinal methods to study depression within adolescent-parent dyads, with the goal of advancing our understanding of interpersonal processes and informing future interventions. In her free time, she enjoys spending time with her dog, listening to music, traveling, practicing yoga, and running.

Join us

Join the CARED Lab

We are looking for curious, motivated people who want to work at the intersection of clinical science and computational methods. You don't need to already be an expert in both; we care most about strong thinking, care for the clinical questions, and willingness to learn.

Prospective graduate students

If you're interested in affect dynamics, digital mental health, or psychotherapy research, get in touch with a CV and a short note about the questions that excite you.

Postdocs

Openings depend on funding, but we're always happy to hear from strong candidates, including those interested in applying for fellowships together.
